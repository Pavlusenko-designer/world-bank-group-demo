Key Challenges

Three structural bottlenecks limiting impact

Challenge 1

Fragmented logistics systems

Transport, customs, and planning platforms operate in parallel rather than as one connected operating layer.

  • Duplicated workflows across agencies
  • Slow handoffs between port, customs, and inland transport
  • Limited real-time visibility on disruptions

Challenge 2

Low SME digital integration

Many smaller businesses still face onboarding friction and cannot consistently access digital logistics services.

  • High integration cost for small operators
  • Inconsistent digital standards across sub-systems
  • Limited support pathways outside major hubs

Challenge 3

Uneven regional infrastructure impact

Urban centers capture improvements faster while secondary corridors experience weaker utilization and slower service quality gains.

  • Connectivity quality varies sharply by geography
  • Digital service maturity is concentrated in metro regions
  • Outcome measurement remains inconsistent across corridors

Strategic Priorities

From bottlenecks to execution priorities

Priority 1

Unified national logistics and infrastructure data layer

Connect transport, customs, and utilities systems through shared standards and live operational interfaces.

Priority 2

Digital adoption pathways for SMEs

Lower entry barriers with tiered onboarding, interoperability support, and practical workflow tooling.

Priority 3

Outcome-based investment for regional gaps

Target funding to corridors where adoption, service continuity, and utilization remain below benchmark.
